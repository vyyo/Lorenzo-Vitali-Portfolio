Foreword

The main goal with Train Life was to allow the team to focus on original characters and an engaging story. As the project's team leader and lead designer, I've worked on these aspects of Train Life:

Core

  • Conflict: understanding the passengers' personalities and likes
  • The player must get off the train before their social battery is depleted
  • The player can talk to the passengers to either convince them to move or ask for their help

Persuasion

  • Replies cost energy, based on the their weight and the passenger’s reaction
  • Different replies lead to different dialogue tree branches
  • Selecting the correct replies persuades the passenger, making them move
  • Some replies are unlocked through in-environment dialogues/interactions

Narrative Pacing

  • Pacing: alternates between serious/tense and comedic/relaxed moments, both in between chapters and within the chapter itself
  • Structure: 7 chapters(days), composed of 3 phases each

Gameplay Loop

  • Interlude: contemplative phase, introductory text and still shots
  • Morning: main phase, new passengers and persuasions
  • Night: relaxation phase, energy recharge and retrospective on the day

Writing: Day One

  • Goal: narrative (insecurities, relationship with grandpa) and gameplay (first loop, persuasion) introduction; conveying the game’s comedic tone
  • Pacing: low-tension, alternates between serious(interlude, night) and comedic (morning) moments
  • Characters: Angy and Mr Rissues, secondary comic reliefs, shift the focus on grandpa’s night dialogue

Narrative Supervision

Periodic revisions of the other narrative designers’ work, verifying:

  • Grammar/Spelling mistakes
  • Character lexical coherence
  • Dialogue tree coherence, in relation to narrative goals
  • Dialogue tree energy cost balancing

Soundtrack

Musical composition of tracks capable of conveying the game’s moods and settings, through the analysis of compatible musical references.

Engine Tasks

Routine engine-related tasks:

  • Blockout
  • UI Setup
  • Animations and animator controllers setup
  • Dialogue trees implementation (tool: Articy)
  • Cutscene scripts and timelines
  • Testing

Team Management

Routine team-related tasks:

  • GDD
  • Presentations
  • Weekly meetings
  • Task assignment/Task progress supervision
  • 3D/Concept artists support
  • Programmers support (scripting, debugging)
